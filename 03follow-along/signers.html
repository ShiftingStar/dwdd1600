Who Were the Signers

Famous Signers

Some of the most famous signers of the Declaration of Independence include:

Thomas Jefferson
Thomas Jefferson, a delegate from Virginia, was the principle author of the Declaration of Independence. He later served as the 3rd President of the United States and is remembered for his contributions to American ideals of liberty and democracy.
John Adams
John Adams, representing Massachusetts, was a vocal advocate for independence in the Continental Congress. He became the 2nd President of the United States and played a key role in drafting foundational documents and promoting the cause of freedom.
Benjamin Franklin
Representing Pennsylvania, Franklin was a renowned statesman, inventor, and diplomat, highly respected both in America and abroad.
John Hancock
As the President of the Second Continental Congress, Hancock's bold and prominent signature is one of the most recognizable on the Declaration.
Samuel Adams
A cousin of John Adams and an influential revolutionary leader from Massachusetts, Samuel Adams was instrumental in organizing resistance to British policies.
Richard Henry Lee
A Virignian who proposed the the resolution for independence, his motion set the stage for drafting the Declaration.
Roger Sherman
Representing Connecticut, Sherman was the only person to sign all four major founding documents of the U.S.: the Declaration of Independence, the Articles of Confederation, the Constitution, and the Articles of Association.
Robert Morris
A financer from Pennsylvania who played a critical role in funding the Revolutionary War, Morris later signed the Constitution as well.

Each of the individuals played a significant role in the founding of the United States and is remembered for their courage in signing the Declaration, an act considered treasonous by the British Crown.
